Phase 3: Threat Modeling

In the real world, this is where you sit down and figure out the easiest way to break in based on what you found in Reconnaissance.

What to do: You don't need to run any tools here. You just look at your Nmap results and make a plan. You see a web server (Port 80) and an SSH portal (Port 2222). You know from the RoE that brute-forcing SSH is forbidden, so the web server is your primary attack vector.

What to Document (The "Threat Modeling" Section): Add this brief section to your report:

  1. Primary Vector: "The primary attack surface was identified as the Modern Bank web application hosted on Port 80. As a custom web application, it holds the highest probability for logic flaws and injection vulnerabilities."

  1. Secondary Vector: "The SSH service on Port 2222 (identified as a potential honeypot/management port) was noted as a secondary vector, though strict RoE limitations prohibit automated credential brute-forcing."
